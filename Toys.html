Where it starts

If you're reading this at 2:47 a.m. with a screaming baby on your shoulder, I see you.

Somewhere around month four, I started keeping a running list on my phone — not of milestones, but of what actually worked when he was overtired, overstimulated, or mid-teething-meltdown in the cereal aisle.

Quick Answers

Questions I Got Asked in Every Mom Group I Joined

How do I know if my baby is actually teething or just fussy?

In our house, the giveaways were extra drool, chewing on everything (including us), and a slight uptick in fussiness or disrupted naps. Every baby is different, so if you're ever worried about fever or unusual symptoms, it's always worth a quick call to your pediatrician rather than guessing.

What actually helped the most during the worst teething nights?

For us, it was less about one miracle product and more about rotation — having a few different textures on hand (a mitten, a sensory toy, the long silicone sticks) so there was always something cool and clean ready to go, day or night.

Is it normal for babies to prefer remotes, phones, and keys over toys?

Extremely normal, and honestly kind of sweet — they want to do what you do. A shape-alike teether was our low-effort way of giving him "his own remote" so ours could survive the year.

When should I introduce open-ended toys like building tiles?

We started around two, once he had the patience to stack more than three blocks without immediately knocking them down out of pure joy. Every kid's timeline looks different, so let their interest lead.
