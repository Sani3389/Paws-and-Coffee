Questions I Get Asked All the Time

Do I really need all five of these at once?

Nope. Babies and toddlers need different things at different stages. If you're in the newborn fog, start with the swing and the lovey. Once teeth and boredom show up, the teether, and busy activities earn their keep.

Are these safe for newborns specifically?

The swing, lovey, and teether are all designed with young infants in mind, but always check the manufacturer's age and weight guidelines on the listing before buying, and follow safe sleep and supervised play practices.

What made you pick these over everything else out there?

Honestly, staying power. These are the items that survived past week two in our house, got used daily, and didn't end up in a donation bin. That's the bar I hold everything to now.
