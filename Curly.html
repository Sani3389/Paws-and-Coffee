THE BACKSTORY

CURLY GIRL FAQ

Questions I get asked in every DM

Why does my hair frizz the second I step outside?

Curly strands are naturally more porous, so they absorb moisture from humid air faster than straight hair — the cuticle swells and the frizz halo shows up. An anti-humidity, heat-protectant spray creates a light barrier so your curl pattern holds instead of expanding into a cloud.

Do I really need a separate mousse AND cream AND oil?

Each one is doing a different job: mousse gives lift and cushion at the root, cream defines and seals the cuticle, and oil locks moisture in on the ends. Skipping one usually shows up as either flat roots or frizzy, dry ends — the classic curly-girl combo nobody wants.

What's the point of diffusing instead of air-drying?

Air-drying is gentler but slower, and curls left to dry undisturbed for hours are more prone to frizz from friction and movement. A diffuser cups your curls as they dry, which speeds things up while keeping the defined shape.
