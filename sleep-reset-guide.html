A Guide to Better Sleep

The Sleep Reset: A Season of Small, Deliberate Changes

I'm Olivia. For the better part of a year, I was tired in a way that regular sleep didn't seem to fix. Here's exactly what I changed, piece by piece, and what actually made a difference.

Where It Started

Eight hours in bed. Still exhausted by 10 a.m.

I used to think "getting enough sleep" was purely a matter of hours — go to bed early enough, wake up late enough, and the math should work out. It didn't. I'd lie awake overheated at midnight, wake at 3 a.m. tangled in sheets that felt more like sandpaper than fabric, and still drag myself out of bed feeling like I hadn't slept at all.

I live just outside Denver, in a house with more temperature swings than I'd like to admit, and at some point I stopped accepting "that's just how sleep is now" as an answer. Over about four months, I replaced one piece of my bed at a time and paid close attention to what actually changed how I slept — not what a marketing page promised, but what genuinely made a difference by night three, night ten, night thirty.

"Nobody tells you that the hardest part of fixing your sleep isn't finding time to rest — it's realizing your bed has been quietly working against you the whole time."

This is that project, laid out in the order I tackled it. If you're lying awake tonight overheated, restless, or just perpetually unrefreshed, I hope this saves you the four months of trial and error it took me.

Quick Answers

Questions Worth Asking Before You Buy

Why am I still exhausted even after a full eight hours?

For me, it came down to the sleep environment itself — overheating, an outdated setup, and a nervous system that hadn't caught up with a stressful season. Fixing the physical variables (temperature, weight, texture) made a bigger difference than expected, though ongoing fatigue is always worth mentioning to a doctor too.

Do weighted blankets actually work, or is it a placebo?

I was skeptical too. What I can say honestly is that the even pressure noticeably calmed that wired, anxious feeling for both my partner and me — though everyone's nervous system responds differently, so it's worth trying one with a solid return policy.

Is a silk pillowcase actually worth it, or just a nice-to-have?

For us it became a genuine routine fix rather than a luxury — less hair tangling, less friction on skin, and a cooler surface on warm nights. A small, low-effort change that quietly added up.

What should I change first if I can only afford one thing right now?

If overheating is the main issue at night, start with the sheets — it was the single biggest change in this whole process. Everything else was built on top of that.
